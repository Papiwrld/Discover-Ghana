Historical Sites

Explore Ghana's rich history through its magnificent castles, forts, and monuments that tell stories of kingdoms, trade, and the struggle for independence.

UNESCO Site

Cape Coast Castle

Built in 1653 by Swedish traders, Cape Coast Castle stands as one of the most significant monuments of the transatlantic slave trade. This UNESCO World Heritage Site offers visitors a profound and emotional journey through history. The castle's dungeons, "Door of No Return," and museum provide invaluable insights into the African diaspora.

Cape Coast, Central Region
Open: 9AM - 5PM Daily GH₵ 40
UNESCO Site

Elmina Castle (St. George's Castle)

The oldest European building in sub-Saharan Africa, Elmina Castle was established by the Portuguese in 1482. Originally built for gold trade, it later became a major hub for the slave trade. The castle's whitewashed walls and strategic location make it an architectural marvel and a powerful historical landmark.

Elmina, Central Region
Open: 9AM - 5PM Daily GH₵ 40
Historical

Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Park

Located on the site where Ghana declared independence on March 6, 1957, this park honors Dr. Kwame Nkrumah, Ghana's first President and a champion of Pan-Africanism. The memorial features his mausoleum, a museum with personal artifacts, and beautifully landscaped gardens.

Accra, Greater Accra
Open: 9AM - 6PM Daily GH₵ 20
Cultural

Manhyia Palace Museum

The official residence of the Asantehene (King of the Ashanti), Manhyia Palace in Kumasi offers a glimpse into the rich heritage of the Ashanti Kingdom. The museum displays royal regalia, historical artifacts, and tells the story of the Ashanti people and their kings.

Kumasi, Ashanti Region
Open: 9AM - 5PM (Closed Sundays) GH₵ 30

Nature & Wildlife

Experience Ghana's incredible biodiversity, from tropical rainforests with canopy walkways to vast savannahs teeming with wildlife.

Top Attraction

Kakum National Park

Home to Ghana's only canopy walkway, Kakum National Park offers a unique perspective of the tropical rainforest from 30 meters above the ground. The park covers 375 square kilometers and is home to forest elephants, various primates, and over 400 butterfly species.

Central Region
Open: 8AM - 5PM GH₵ 60 (with walkway)
Safari

Mole National Park

Ghana's largest wildlife refuge spanning 4,840 square kilometers. Home to elephants, antelopes, baboons, warthogs, and over 300 bird species. The park offers walking safaris, jeep tours, and stunning views from the Mole Motel perched on a ridge overlooking waterholes.

Savannah Region
Open: 6AM - 6PM GH₵ 50
Nature

Wli Waterfalls

The highest waterfall in West Africa, Wli Falls cascades down from the Ghana-Togo border mountains. The trek to the lower falls takes about 45 minutes through lush vegetation, while the upper falls require a more challenging 3-hour hike. Home to thousands of fruit bats.

Hohoe, Volta Region
45min - 3hr hike GH₵ 25
Gardens

Aburi Botanical Gardens

Established in 1890, these gardens offer a peaceful retreat in the Akuapem Ridge mountains. Features century-old trees, diverse plant species, and cool mountain climate. Popular for picnics, nature walks, and photography.

Aburi, Eastern Region
Open: 6AM - 6PM GH₵ 15
Wildlife

Shai Hills Resource Reserve

Just 55km from Accra, this reserve features rocky hills, grasslands, and forests. Home to baboons, antelopes, and various bird species. Visitors can explore ancient caves that once served as Shai people's hideouts.

Greater Accra Region
Open: 6AM - 5PM GH₵ 20
Eco-Tourism

Bunso Arboretum

A 17-hectare ecological paradise featuring a canopy walkway, butterfly sanctuary, and herb garden. Home to over 110 bird species, giant snails, and beautiful orchids. Perfect for eco-tourism and educational visits.

Eastern Region
Open: 8AM - 5PM GH₵ 30

Beaches & Coastal Areas

Relax on Ghana's beautiful Atlantic coastline, from vibrant beach resorts to serene hidden coves.

Popular

Labadi Beach

Accra's most popular beach destination, known for its lively atmosphere, beach bars, and weekend entertainment. Perfect for experiencing local beach culture with live music and horse rides.

Accra, Greater Accra GH₵ 10
Surfing

Busua Beach

A surfer's paradise and one of Ghana's most beautiful beaches. This laid-back fishing village offers excellent waves, beachfront accommodations, and stunning sunsets over the Atlantic.

Western Region Great for Surfing
Water Sports

Ada Foah

Where the Volta River meets the Atlantic Ocean. Famous for water sports, kite surfing, and the Aqua Safari Resort. Experience the unique ecosystem where freshwater and saltwater merge.

Greater Accra Region Boat Tours Available

Travel Tips for Ghana

Make the most of your Ghanaian adventure with these helpful tips.

Best Time to Visit

November to March offers dry weather and is ideal for wildlife viewing and beach activities.

Currency

The Ghana Cedi (GH₵) is the local currency. ATMs are widely available in major cities.

Language

English is the official language. Local languages include Twi, Ga, Ewe, and many others.

Health

Yellow fever vaccination required. Malaria prophylaxis recommended. Drink bottled water.
