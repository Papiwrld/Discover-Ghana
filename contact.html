Essential Travel Tips

Prepare for your Ghana adventure with these helpful tips.

Visa Requirements

Most visitors need a visa. Apply online through the Ghana Immigration Service or at your nearest embassy. Processing takes 5-10 business days.

What to Pack

Light, breathable clothing, sunscreen, insect repellent, comfortable walking shoes, and a light rain jacket for the rainy season (April-July).

Health & Safety

Yellow fever vaccination required. Malaria prophylaxis recommended. Ghana is one of Africa's safest countries, but take normal precautions.

Currency & Payments

Getting There

Kotoka International Airport (ACC) in Accra is the main entry point. Direct flights from major cities worldwide. Airport transfers available.

Getting Around

Domestic flights, private taxis, ride-sharing apps (Uber, Bolt), and tro-tros (minibuses) available. Car rental with driver recommended for tourists.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about visiting Ghana.

What is the best time to visit Ghana?

November to March offers dry, sunny weather ideal for most activities. However, Ghana is a year-round destination with each season offering unique experiences.

Is Ghana safe for tourists?

Yes! Ghana is considered one of the safest and most stable countries in Africa with a welcoming population. Normal travel precautions apply.

What languages are spoken?

English is the official language and widely spoken. Local languages include Twi, Ga, Ewe, and Hausa among many others.

How many days should I spend in Ghana?

We recommend 7-10 days to explore the highlights. 2 weeks allows for a more relaxed pace and visits to northern Ghana.
